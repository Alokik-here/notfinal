Journey To Independence

The incredible journey of the freedom struggle of India from a huge revolution in 1857 to celebrating freedom after years of struggle in 1947 is highly fascinating. Plethora of important events occurred during these 90 years, several wins and some defeats, but one day, to everyone's surprise, these little contributions added up to a drastic change, and people won the liberty which they cherished.

Many books have been published on the freedom struggles and the incidents that occurred in between. Some were written by freedom fighters, while others were written about them. While these works continue to inspire many Indians even today, many of them were penned by the independence warriors when they were imprisoned. Among the best recommendations of all time are:

List of books

Gitanjali:
Published in 1910 : by Rabindranath Tagore: A collection of his renowned prose poetry translated into English. its is about being pious to God and focuses on the concept that God is present everywhere.



Hind Swaraj:
Published in 1909 : by MK Gandhi: It is based on Indian home rule. Hind Swaraj sketches forth the broad contours of a multi - ethnic society - a new civilizational framework in its most basic form.



Unhappy India:
Published in 1928 by Lala Lajpat Rai: In this book, Lala Lajpat Rai reacted to Katherine Mayo, a journalist who produced a prejudiced book on India that focused solely on its difficulties. Rai compared Indian society to that of the United States and Britain at the time, pointing out that their situations are not unlike to India's.



Hind Swaraj:
Published in 1946: by Rajendra Prasad: The alteration in political borders involved the split of two British Indian provinces, Bengal and Punjab. The primarily Muslim districts in these provinces were allocated to Pakistan, whereas the majority non-Muslim districts were awarded to India. It mostly discusses how India was preparing for independence and democratic governance.
